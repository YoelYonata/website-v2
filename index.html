NETGEAR Software Test Engineer

NETGEAR

For my first coop job, I had the chance to work at Netgear Canada. Netgear is a globally known company for their computer networking products. The Netgear in the Richmond location was a part of the aircard department of Netgear. Aircards are portable mobile hotspots that attaches to 4G LTE network and shares wifi connection to nearby devices.

There were two main projects that were under development when I was working at Netgear, MR2100 and M5F. The MR2100 is a Gigabit LTE mobile router that supports up to 2Gbps speeds. I spent most of my time working on the M5F, which is the world's first mobile hotspot developed for AT&T. As a Software Test Engineer, my main responsibilites include both software testing and firmware testing.Software testing include testing display bugs and UI issues. I performed mostly firmware testing on the M5F during my work term. Firmware testing include band combination testing, wifi throughput testing and stability testing.

Through my work term at Netgear there are lots of takeaways and skills I have acquired. I performed many regression testings as a part of my job. I had the opportunities of using their lab tools and also some python programming to create automated test scripts. Even though I have learned some of these skills from school, I believe working at Netgear further improves my proficiency and adds a lot more to my knowledge. Working at Netgear has taught me a lot about working under pressure, multitasking and having extra attention to details.
